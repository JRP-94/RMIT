Requirements of a Penetration tester

Becoming a Penetration tester has many paths, but most employers looks for the following things:

- A bachelor or masters in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Information Technology or any relevant field.
- For junior positions, 1-3 years experience in the IT indu
- Certifications like Ethical Hacker Certification or Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)or similar

These are considered the bare minimum and often employers require more, like strong command of multiple Operating systems (including Kali linux), experience with multiple different programming languages like C, C++, PHP, Python etc. The language requirements can vary from employer to employer. The position also requires Social Engineering skills as humans are often the most vulnerable part of security. Knowledge in software, firmware and hardware design are considered desirable, as well as networking and cryptography. On top of all this, experience with multiple essential tools like wireshark (wifi packet sniffer), Metasploit (exploit program) etc are a must.

In addition to the technical side, as an individual you must have excellent communication skills to relay complex topics to your employer in the debrief as well as working efficiently and effectively in a team environment to achieve the common goal. The position also demands of the individual strong time management skills, excellent writing skills (for reports for the debrief), creativity, initiative and importantly integrity as you are working with highly sensitive information (if for the government a security clearance is often required).

Project Idea: House-hold solar hydroponic garden

Overview

My project is a Fully automated solar hydroponic garden aimed to be marketed at the public. I will be fully self suficent and only require the ower to harvest the vegetables and add the fertaliser when the tank becomes empty. It will know when to empty the water going through, it will monitor and control fertiliser concentrations in the solution, the system will monitor and charge a battery cell connected to the device so that it runs over night. It will have LED grow lights to maximise yield with the least amount of work. It will aim to reduce the environmental damage of the agricultural industry, reduce the living cost per household, promote healthy eating with fresh food available directly at home.

Motivation

Currently, we live in an unsustainable world where almost every action we take, every little thing we buy creates a larger carbon (and other greenhouse gases) footprint than the earth can recycle. The agricultural industry is one of the biggest contributors to our current climate crisis. This not only includes the expulsion of methane, nitrogen oxide and carbon dioxide, it also includes mass deforestation causing population decline of many native Australian flora and fauna species. its also the poisoning of our vital waterways by the run off of fertiliser, creating algal blooms of cyanobacteria which deplete the water of oxygen and collapse complete ecosystems in the waterways. The motivation of the project is to reduce these devasting effects by putting the power in the hands of the citizens and creating less of a demand, therefore reduce the industry and its impact on the planet.

Description

The hydroponic system will be free standing and have one PVC pipe zigzag from top to bottom, this will be the plant ladder. A water pump with push the solution of water and soluble fertiliser through a clear plastic tube to the top of the PVC piping from a storage tank. Gravity will then pull the solution down the pipe and running gently over the root system of your plants back into the storage tank. The storage tank will be constantly monitoring the solution concentration and adjusting it for optimal yields. The system requires that is position in an area that receives adequate sunlight.

The whole system will be encased in a clear plastic container, then a sensor will monitor air temp and moisture and adjust it accordingly by opening and closing vents in the case. When the sun sets or when the system is receiving limited sunlight (a sensor is responsible for detection), the LED grow lights will activate and a light shutter will encase the whole system, this will allow for maximum absorption by the plants and reduce wasted energy.

After the optimal light exposure has been reached the LEDs will turn off and the shutters will retracted. All the electrical components will be connected to a battery cell and an external solar panel. The solar panel with charge the batteries and run the pump during the day and when the voltage of the panel drops too low it will switch to using the battery cell. When the plants are ready to be harvested, the owner will be notified via the mobile app as to which plant exactly is ready, each plant with have a slot allocation.

To set up the system, the user will use the app to select the appropriate plants for the time of year and which plants grow best in the same conditions to optimise yields and limit (if any) wastes. The user will then place the plant into the allocated slot indicated by the app (and by a flashing Indicator LED) and close the system. The tank system will compromise of the main storage tank where the water and fertiliser solution is contained and connected to the main tank will be a tank containing the fertiliser for the system to use when adjusting the concentrations.

In addition to the fertiliser tank being connected to the main tank, the main tank will be connected to a fresh water supply. When the tank needs to be cleaned, the app will notify the user and the user will initiated the cleaning program via the app. The pump will stop, the user will switch the pumps output from into the system to the drainage hose. The user will then connect the drainage hose to the pump and to wherever the user would like to dispose of the waste (preferably to an outside garden). Once ready, the user will click on a button in the app and it will go until its finished. When finished the user will be prompted again with the reverse of what they just did.

Skills required

In addition to all the parts listed above, the hydroponic system will require multiple different programs to be written for the front end and the back end. These include software to run all the sensors and then calculate the required adjustments for the system, then a program that takes this information and makes the adjustments in the system, like switching to the battery supply when the luxmeter reading is too low or when the sun has set, activate the blinds and the LED lights turn on. An operating system on the central control system (Raspberry Pi) is also required, a compatible mobile application and a program to communicate with the server containing the database required for the whole hydroponic system itself, storage of data and for application.

As the design of the system isn’t too complex and designed to be simple and straight forward the programs will follow suit. This should makes the project more feasible and likely to decrease the initial upfront cost and should increase adoption

Outcome

It will hopefully provide households with fresh vegetables in a clean and efficient manner. It will be carbon neutral as it will run completely off solar energy. It will be fully automated and the only required input by the user is simple and cheap in hopes to increase adoption. In the future, the plan is to put the idea forward to the government and negotiate a subsidy scheme mirroring the solar energy scheme. In addition to addressing key environmental impacts it will also encourage healthier eating in every household that adopts this technology, addressing our obesity issue in Australia and in a trickle down kind of effect, reduce the strain on the healthcare system by lifestyle related diseases like diabetes and obesity, and free up funds and resources for other issues we face in Australia with an aging population.
